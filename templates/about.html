Confidence System

How reliability is measured.

Reliability Band

The reliability score is not a single model metric — it's a composite of classifier confidence and benchmark alignment, weighted to reflect the most informative signal.

reliability = (top_classifier_prob × 0.6) + (alignment% × 0.4)
High≥ 75%
Moderate55 – 74%
Exploratory< 55%

Benchmark Alignment

Each of the three inputs is compared against an empirically-derived optimal band. Alignment is the percentage of inputs that sit within their target range, contributing to overall confidence and optimization recommendations.

Honest Limitations

Where the model has edges.

⚠️
Predictions are probabilistic, not deterministic

Score forecasts represent a statistical expectation based on similar observed profiles — not a guarantee. Real outcomes include factors the model cannot observe.

🔄
Boundary condition profiles have lower confidence

Students with very low or very high values on a single metric often sit near classifier boundaries where small changes can flip the predicted class. The reliability band accounts for this.

📊
Only three features are used

Study hours, attendance, and participation are powerful behavioral signals but incomplete. Factors like subject difficulty, external stress, test anxiety, and learning style are not captured.

🌐
Dataset demographics may not generalize perfectly

The training data represents a particular slice of academic environments. Profiles from significantly different institutions or curricula may be less well represented.
